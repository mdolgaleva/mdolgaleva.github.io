About

Maria Dolgaleva

I hold a degree in philology and advanced my training in simultaneous interpreting at a United Nations program in Moscow, Russia. I also studied specialized translation at the University of Bologna in Italy. After relocating to the United States, I became a licensed court interpreter by passing the National Center for State Courts (NCSC) exam with a Master designation, permitting me to interpret in court proceedings in all courts in the state.

Language has always been more than a profession for me — it's a lifelong passion. I’m deeply engaged with words, contexts, and the subtle connections that shape meaning across cultures and legal systems.
